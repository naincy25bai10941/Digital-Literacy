## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
UPI fraud has become quite common these
days, especially among people who use online
payment apps regularly. It is a type of
cybercrime where a person is tricked into
sending money or sharing sensitive details like
OTP or PIN.

A common way this happens is through fake
messages or calls. For example, a person may
receive a message saying that they have won a
prize or that a payment is pending. The
message usually contains a link or asks the
person to scan a QR code. Once the person
clicks the link or scans the code, they are
asked to enter their details. In some cases, the
fraudster calls and pretends to be from a bank
or customer care service and convinces the
person to share OTP or approve a payment
request. As soon as the details are shared,
money gets deducted from the account.

Students, elderly people, and even regular
users who are not very careful online are often
targeted. The main reason is lack of awareness
and urgency created by the fraudster.

The consequences can be serious, such as loss
of money, stress, and difficulty in recovering
the amount. In some cases, people also trust in
online payment systems after such incidents.
